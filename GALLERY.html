☛ Pichai Sundararajan (born June 10, 1972), better known as Sundar Pichai, is an American business executive. He is the chief executive officer (CEO) of Alphabet Inc. and its subsidiary Google. As Google’s Chief Executive Officer, responsible for product development and technology strategy, as well as the company's day-to-day-operations. Joined Google in 2004 and helped lead the development of Google Toolbar and Google Chrome, key consumer products which are now used by over 1 billion people. In 2014, took over product, engineering and research efforts for all of Google’s products and platforms. This includes Search, Maps, Communications, Google Play, Commerce, and Ads, as well as suite of cloud-based solutions for consumers, businesses and education known as Google Apps and Cloud Platform. It also includes two computing platforms – Android and Chrome. After years of working closely with Google’s co-founders, Larry Page and Sergey Brin, became Google’s Chief Executive Officer in August 2015. Joined the Board of Directors of Alphabet, Google's parent company, in July 2017. Before joining Google, held positions at Applied Materials and McKinsey & Company. Bachelor's degree, Indian Institute of Technology, recipient, Institute Silver Medal; Master's degree, Stanford University; MBA, Siebel Scholar and Palmer Scholar, Wharton School.
